Mercedes Sosa

Greatest exponent of Argentine folklore.

Here's a time line of Mercedes Sosa life:

  • 1935 -Born in Tucuman, Argentine.
  • 1957 - She moved to Mendoza after marrying the musician Oscar Matus, with whom she had her son, Fabián Matus.
  • 1961 - Recorded his first album.
  • 1966 - He got his first hit, Samba para no morir.
  • 1967 - First tour of Europe touring Holland, Belgium, Switzerland and Germany.
  •    ´70 - During the Latin American dictatorships, he sang with Violeta Parra. Her song "Gracias a la vida" became a huge hit.
  • 1978 - Her husband died and a few months later the Argentine authorities prohibited her from singing in public, so she decided to settle in Paris.
  • 1979 - Recorded an album in Portuguese and three years later he returned to Argentina filling football stadiums.
  • 1982 - She was able to sing again before the Argentine public at the Opera Theater in Buenos Aires accompanied by the most prominent local musicians such as León Gieco and Charly García.
  • 1984 - Once again gave significant support to the composers of his country with "Vengo a ofrecer mi corazón", in which he recorded the song "Razón de vivir" and "Madre de madres", "Entre a mi pago sin hitar" and "Canción para Carito".
  • 1986 - She toured extensively in Germany and Central Europe, sang in the United States, and also held 25 recitals in Brazil. In the midst of those tours, she edited "Mercedes Sosa '86" and "Mercedes Sosa '87".
  • 1988 - She released "Amigos Míos", a compilation album. In that same year she ventured as a producer, organizing the memorable show "Without Borders". In 1989 she received the medal of the Order of the Commander of Arts and Letters, awarded by the Ministry of Culture of the French Republic.
  • 1992 - In the Golden Room of the Buenos Aires Deliberative Council, she was declared an Illustrious Citizen of Buenos Aires. She was declared an Illustrious Visit of Texas and Houston by the state governor and the city mayor.
  • 1994 - She sang on behalf of America in the second Christmas concert held in the Nervi Hall of the Vatican.She has been declared an Illustrious Citizen of Tucumán, has received the Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany, the Medal of Cultural Merit of Ecuador, the Plaque of the Universidad Nacional Mayor de San Marcos, Peru in recognition of her 30 years of diffusion of the Latin American singing, the 1993 ACE Award for his album "Sino", and the Martín Fierro for best musical show on television.
  • 1996 - In the spring he suffered an aggravation of ailments in his physique. For this reason, with a picture of masked depression, she spent five months in bed, with dehydration that made her lose more than 30 kilos. She was on the verge of death. After half a year of suffering, she returned to sing in a remarkable album, meaningfully titled "Al despertar".
  • 1999 - Fully recovered from her illness, she remains active and touring extensively for the next 10 years.
  • His latest works were "Cantora", a double album where he sings 34 songs as a duet with prominent Latin American singers, closing with the Argentine national anthem; and Cantora II, the second volume of duets that she performed with 35 national and international artists. These two albums are true anthology.
  • 2009 - This extraordinary woman died at the age of 74, in Buenos Aires, on October 4.
